UX/UI Design

Project Relay

This iOS app ignited my decision to sign up for the BLOC UX design course. I had a vision for the design, but I needed to learn how a UX professional would approach it.

Here are some of the major milestones throughout the design journey.

landing page

THE PROBLEM

How do you send mobile content to people?

When I find content online browsing on my phone, I’ve never had the right way to share it. I didn’t want to post it to Facebook or tweet it. If I found a cool article online and wanted to share it with some friends, my dad and sister, and my roommates, I didn’t have an easy way to do that.

I knew there could be a better way to share things quickly without having to mass text or email it.

GATHERING DATA

I created a Google form to ask friends and family how they are sharing content on mobile devices. This would tell me if my theory was right.

The responses told me I was. There wasn’t a consistent tool people were using to share content with select individuals.

THE IDENTITY

Coming up with name and logo was the biggest challenge for me. I had to create a name that could be used as both a noun and a verb.

Relay stuck because it captured the core action of the app and it met my naming requirements-- I could ‘relay’ and news article or I could receive a ‘relay’ from a friend.

TARGET USERS

I started thinking about the different types of people would use Relay. I expected the majority of users to be teenages and young adults in their 20's. However, my goal was design the app so my grandmother could use it.

I created different user personas to vet the requirements including a young teenager, college student, young professional, dad, and grandma.

WIREFRAMING

My intent was a simple, clean, easy-to-use interface. I wanted to adhere to iOS guidelines so users would intuitively be able to navigate around without any tutorials.

DESIGN MOCKUP

I used only four colors on my palette for the design. Building off of the blue I had used for the logo, I chose a brighter green to indicate action on the screen. The biggest addition that wasn't included in the wireframes was having icons idicate the type of messages in the inbox.
